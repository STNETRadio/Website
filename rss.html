About STNET Radio RSS

We tested self-hosted podcasting via rss.stnetradio.com with some of our podcasts. The results showed smoother playback and significantly improved audio quality.

STNET Radio RSS was developed to address these issues, allowing our creators to upload high-quality audio files for their listeners seamlessly.

Why self-host?

More control over delivery, fewer compression surprises, and consistent listener experience.

Better sound

Serve high-bitrate files with smart caching for crisp voices and punchy music.

Simple pipelines

Upload once, publish everywhere via open RSS. No walled gardens.

FAQ

What is an RSS feed?

RSS is an open format for distributing updates. Podcast apps can subscribe to an RSS URL to fetch new episodes automatically.

Will existing podcast links still work?

Yes. We’re setting redirects for legacy feeds so your app continues to receive updates without manual changes.

Why does audio sound better now?

Self-hosting lets us control encoding and delivery, avoiding extra re-compression and preserving more detail.
